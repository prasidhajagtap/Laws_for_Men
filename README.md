## 1. New Laws 2024 — BNS, BNSS, BSA

### What Changed on July 1, 2024

Three new laws completely replaced India's 160-year-old colonial criminal justice framework.

| New Code | Full Name | Replaced |
|---|---|---|
| **BNS** | Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 | Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860 |
| **BNSS** | Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 | Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973 |
| **BSA** | Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 | Indian Evidence Act, 1872 |

### Which Law Applies to Your Case?

```
Case filed BEFORE July 1, 2024
  └─ IPC + CrPC + Indian Evidence Act apply

Case filed ON OR AFTER July 1, 2024
  └─ BNS + BNSS + BSA apply
```

> ✅ **The punishments and definitions are mostly the same.**  
> ❗ **The section numbers are different.** Always confirm with your lawyer.

---

### BNS — Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (Replaced IPC)

Defines **what is a crime** in India.

**Key sections affecting men:**

| Old IPC | New BNS | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| § 498A | § 85 | Cruelty by husband or relatives |
| § 406 | § 316 | Criminal breach of trust |
| § 306 | § 108 | Abetment of suicide |
| § 182 | § 216 | False information to police |
| § 211 | § 250 | Filing false criminal charge |
| § 499/500 | § 356/357 | Defamation |
| § 420 | § 318 | Cheating |

---

### BNSS — Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (Replaced CrPC)

Defines **how crimes are investigated, tried, and how bail works.**

**Key sections affecting men:**

| Old CrPC | New BNSS | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| § 41A | § 35 | Police notice before arrest |
| § 438 | § 482 | Anticipatory Bail |
| § 482 | § 528 | High Court power to quash FIR |
| § 125 | § 144 | Maintenance |
| § 167 | § 187 | Remand / police custody |

**New protections under BNSS:**

- 🛡️ Police **must issue notice (§ 35)** before arresting you for offences up to 3 years
- ⏱️ Police custody capped at **15 days** for most offences
- 📋 Chargesheet must be filed within **60 days** (extendable to 90) — else you get default bail
- ⚖️ Trial must complete in **3–7 years** — else bail is available automatically

---

### BSA — Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (Replaced Evidence Act)

Defines **what counts as evidence** in court.

**Biggest change — Electronic Evidence is now PRIMARY:**

```
Under BSA § 63:
  WhatsApp chats      ✅ Primary evidence
  Emails              ✅ Primary evidence
  Call recordings     ✅ Primary evidence
  SMS messages        ✅ Primary evidence
  CCTV footage        ✅ Primary evidence
  Bank statements     ✅ Primary evidence
  Location data       ✅ Primary evidence
```

> ⚠️ **Important:** To submit electronic evidence in court, you must prepare a  
> **Certificate of Electronic Record under BSA § 63(4).**  
> Your lawyer must do this correctly — without it, evidence can be challenged.

**What to collect RIGHT NOW:**

```
Evidence Checklist
==================
[ ] WhatsApp chats — Export as PDF (include media)
[ ] Emails — Save as PDF with full headers
[ ] Call recordings — Store with date/time metadata
[ ] Photos/videos — Do NOT edit (EXIF data matters)
[ ] Bank statements — Download from bank app
[ ] Google Maps timeline — Export location history
[ ] Social media screenshots — Include timestamps
```

## 2. Section 498A IPC / BNS § 85 — Cruelty

### What the Law Says (Simple Language)

> This law makes it a crime for a **husband or his relatives** to be cruel to a wife.  
> "Cruelty" includes physical harm, mental harassment, or demanding dowry.

The law was created to protect genuine victims. However, it has become **one of the most misused laws in India.** The Supreme Court itself has called it a tool of "legal terrorism" in some cases.

**Over 70% of 498A trials end in acquittal.**

---

### Your Rights Under 498A / BNS § 85

- ✅ Police **cannot arrest you immediately** — must follow Arnesh Kumar Guidelines (2014)
- ✅ You can apply for **Anticipatory Bail (BNSS § 482)** before arrest
- ✅ Your **parents and siblings cannot be arrested** without specific individual evidence
- ✅ You can **file a counter-complaint** for false information (BNS § 216)
- ✅ You can **quash the FIR** at the High Court (BNSS § 528) if it is false

---

### Step-by-Step: What to Do

```
Step 1 — Gather evidence immediately (Day 1)
  ├─ WhatsApp chats, emails, call recordings
  ├─ Bank statements, gift receipts
  └─ Store everything in cloud backup

Step 2 — Hire a criminal lawyer and file Anticipatory Bail (Day 1–2)
  ├─ File under BNSS § 482 at Sessions Court
  └─ This prevents arrest while case is heard

Step 3 — Respond to police notice with a lawyer
  ├─ Do NOT go alone
  ├─ Do NOT sign anything without reading
  └─ Cite BNSS § 35 — police must issue notice, not arrest directly

Step 4 — File counter-complaint if FIR is false
  ├─ BNS § 216 — false information to police
  └─ BNS § 250 — false criminal charge

Step 5 — Attend ALL court dates
  └─ Missing a date = non-bailable warrant (NBW)

Step 6 — Apply for FIR quashing at High Court
  └─ File under BNSS § 528 (old CrPC § 482)
```

---

### Key Warning

> ⚠️ Even though SC guidelines moderate arrests, some police still arrest first.  
> **Get a lawyer the moment you receive ANY notice or summons.**  
> Do NOT ignore it. Do NOT flee. Appear legally with counsel.

## 3. Maintenance — BNSS § 144 / CrPC § 125

### What the Law Says (Simple Language)

> A wife, children, or parents can claim maintenance (monthly money) from a husband  
> who has sufficient income but refuses to support them.

**Courts sometimes set maintenance very high without verifying actual income.** You can challenge this.

---

### Your Rights

| Right | Details |
|---|---|
| Income-based | Maintenance must reflect BOTH parties' income, not just yours |
| Wife earning? | If she has independent income, maintenance can be reduced or denied |
| Wife deserted? | If she left without reason, maintenance can be denied (BNSS § 144(4)) |
| Interim orders | Can be challenged immediately at Sessions Court |
| Remarriage | If wife remarries or lives with another man, maintenance stops |

---

### How to Fight an Unreasonable Maintenance Order

```
Step 1 — Submit your ACTUAL income proof
  ├─ Salary slips (last 3 years)
  ├─ ITR / Form 16
  ├─ Bank statements
  └─ EMI, rent, medical, parents' support — all expenses documented

Step 2 — Investigate wife's income
  ├─ Her ITR / Form 26AS
  ├─ Employment records
  ├─ Business income / rental income
  └─ Social media (lifestyle evidence)

Step 3 — Challenge interim order immediately
  └─ File revision petition at Sessions Court within 15–30 days

Step 4 — Plead willful desertion if applicable
  └─ Under BNSS § 144(4) — wife who left without cause loses maintenance rights
```

> ✅ **Key case:** *Rajnesh v. Neha (2021) 2 SCC 324* — Both parties MUST disclose  
> all income and assets. Courts cannot rely on one side's claims alone.

## 4. Child Custody

### What the Law Says (Simple Language)

> The **only standard** for child custody is the **"best interest of the child."**  
> It is NOT automatically the mother. Fathers have equal parental rights.

**Applicable laws:**
- Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956 (HMGA)
- Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 (GWA)

---

### Father's Rights

| Situation | Your Right |
|---|---|
| No custody yet | Apply for interim custody / visitation immediately |
| Mother has custody | You have a legal right to regular visitation |
| Visitation denied | File for contempt of court |
| Child hidden | File Habeas Corpus at High Court |
| Mother unfit | Evidence of unfitness can shift custody to father |
| Child below 5 | Mother-preference is NOT absolute (Gaurav Nagpal, 2009) |

---

### Step-by-Step: Fight for Your Child

```
Step 1 — File for interim visitation immediately (Week 1)
  └─ Courts routinely grant this to maintain parent-child bond

Step 2 — Document your relationship with the child
  ├─ Photos, videos, school records
  ├─ Medical visit records
  └─ WhatsApp messages with child

Step 3 — Ensure your name is in school/medical records
  └─ Attend parent-teacher meetings — courts look at involvement

Step 4 — Request court-ordered child assessment
  └─ Ask for a child psychologist or home visit report

Step 5 — File contempt if visitation is denied
  └─ Repeated denial can SHIFT custody to the father
```

---

### Key Cases

| Case | Court | What It Held |
|---|---|---|
| Nil Ratan Kundu v. Abhijit Kundu (2008) | Supreme Court | Custody awarded to father based on child's welfare |
| Gaurav Nagpal v. Sumedha Nagpal (2009) | Supreme Court | Mother-preference for under-5 is not absolute |
| Vikram Vir Vohra v. Shalini Bhalla (2010) | Supreme Court | Denying visitation can shift custody to father |

## 5. False FIR & Bail

### What the Law Says (Simple Language)

> If someone files a false FIR against you, you have two main options:  
> 1. **Anticipatory Bail** — prevent arrest before it happens  
> 2. **Quash the FIR** at the High Court — make the case disappear permanently

---

### Your Rights

- ✅ Apply for **Anticipatory Bail (BNSS § 482)** even before arrest
- ✅ High Court can **permanently quash a false FIR (BNSS § 528)**
- ✅ File **counter-FIR** under BNS § 216 (false info) and § 250 (false charge)
- ✅ After acquittal — file **malicious prosecution suit** for damages
- ✅ **Default bail** — if police don't file chargesheet within 60/90 days

---

### Grounds for Quashing FIR (Bhajan Lal Categories)

> *State of Haryana v. Bhajan Lal (1992)* — Supreme Court listed 7 categories  
> where FIRs **must** be quashed:

```
Category 1 — Allegations do not disclose a cognisable offence
Category 2 — Allegations are absurd and inherently improbable
Category 3 — FIR is motivated by malice / personal vendetta
Category 4 — Offence is not disclosed even if allegations taken at face value
Category 5 — Dispute is civil in nature, not criminal
Category 6 — Proceedings are an abuse of process of law
Category 7 — Continuation would waste court time
```

---

### Step-by-Step: Handle a False FIR

```
Step 1 — Apply for Anticipatory Bail IMMEDIATELY (BNSS § 482)
  ├─ File at Sessions Court or High Court
  └─ Attach: evidence FIR is false + personal background

Step 2 — Collect alibi/innocence evidence
  ├─ Location data, CCTV, witnesses
  └─ Messages proving complainant has motive

Step 3 — File quashing petition at High Court (BNSS § 528)
  ├─ Request stay of arrest along with petition
  └─ Cite relevant Bhajan Lal category

Step 4 — File counter-FIR in parallel
  ├─ BNS § 216 — false information to police
  └─ BNS § 250 — false criminal charge

Step 5 — If charge-sheeted, go through trial
  └─ Cross-examine every witness, challenge every document
```

> ⚠️ **Never flee or ignore summons.** Absconding makes courts assume guilt.  
> Appear legally with counsel every time.
