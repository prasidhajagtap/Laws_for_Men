India's New Criminal Laws

Three landmark codes replaced 160-year-old colonial laws on July 1, 2024. If you are in any legal case filed after that date, these are the laws that apply to you.

Replaced IPC 1860
Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita
BNS, 2023 · Act 45 of 2023

Defines what is a crime in India. Replaces the Indian Penal Code (IPC) 1860. All criminal offences — assault, cruelty, fraud, cheating — are now defined here.

Key changes for men:
  • 📌 Old Sec 498A (IPC) → Now Sec 85 BNS (cruelty by husband/relatives)
  • 📌 Old Sec 406 (IPC) → Now Sec 316 BNS (criminal breach of trust)
  • 📌 Old Sec 107 (IPC) → Now Sec 108 BNS (abetment)
  • 📌 Old Sec 182 (IPC) → Now Sec 216 BNS (false information to police)
  • 📌 Old Sec 499/500 (IPC) → Now Sec 356/357 BNS (defamation)
  • 📌 Old Sec 415/420 (IPC) → Now Sec 318 BNS (cheating)
Replaced CrPC 1973
Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita
BNSS, 2023 · Act 46 of 2023

Defines how a crime is investigated, tried, and punished. Replaces CrPC 1973. Covers arrest, bail, FIR, trial procedure, and appeals.

Key changes for men:
  • 📌 Old Sec 438 CrPC (Anticipatory Bail) → Now Sec 482 BNSS
  • 📌 Old Sec 482 CrPC (FIR Quashing) → Now Sec 528 BNSS
  • 📌 Old Sec 125 CrPC (Maintenance) → Now Sec 144 BNSS
  • 📌 Old Sec 41A CrPC (Police notice) → Now Sec 35 BNSS
  • 📌 Old Sec 167 CrPC (Remand) → Now Sec 187 BNSS
  • 📌 New: Max 15 days police custody (not 60/90 days) for most offences
Replaced Indian Evidence Act 1872
Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam
BSA, 2023 · Act 47 of 2023

Defines what counts as evidence in an Indian court. Replaces the Indian Evidence Act 1872. Crucially, it now explicitly recognises electronic evidence.

Key changes for men:
  • 📌 WhatsApp chats, emails, call recordings now explicitly admissible as primary evidence
  • 📌 Electronic records treated as original documents (not secondary)
  • 📌 Digital signatures and metadata accepted as proof
  • 📌 CCTV footage, phone location data — stronger evidentiary value
  • 📌 Old Sec 27 IEA (confession to police) → Now Sec 23 BSA — same protection applies
⚠️
What this means for your existing or new case
  • ✅ Cases filed before July 1, 2024 continue under IPC + CrPC + Indian Evidence Act
  • ✅ Cases filed on or after July 1, 2024 are governed by BNS + BNSS + BSA
  • ✅ Section numbers have changed — always confirm with a lawyer which code applies to your case
  • ✅ The substance of the law (what is a crime, what is the punishment) is mostly the same — but the section numbers are different
  • ✅ Anticipatory Bail, Maintenance, Quashing — all still exist, just under new section numbers

🆘 Emergency Legal Help

⚠️
If you have been arrested or are about to be: Do NOT sign anything. Ask for a lawyer immediately. You have the right to remain silent. Read this guide first.
1
Stay calm and do NOT argue with police

Anything you say can be used against you. Politely say: "I want to speak with my lawyer before answering any questions."

2
Call NALSA (National Legal Services Authority)

Dial 15100 — free legal aid is your constitutional right under Article 22.

3
Apply for Anticipatory Bail if not yet arrested

File under Section 438 CrPC before arrest. Get a lawyer to file immediately at the Sessions Court.

4
Inform family — document everything

Write down every name, time, and event. Witnesses matter. Screenshots, call logs, WhatsApp chats — preserve all evidence.
