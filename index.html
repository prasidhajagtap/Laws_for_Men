Effective July 1, 2024

India's New Criminal Laws

BNS replaced IPC. BNSS replaced CrPC. BSA replaced the Evidence Act. If your case was filed after July 1, 2024, these laws apply to you.

Replaced IPC 1860
Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita
BNS · Act 45 of 2023
Defines what is a crime.
  • 📌 498A → § 85 BNS
  • 📌 406 → § 316 BNS
  • 📌 182 → § 216 BNS
  • 📌 211 → § 250 BNS
Replaced CrPC 1973
Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita
BNSS · Act 46 of 2023
Defines how crimes are investigated and how bail works.
  • 📌 Anticipatory Bail → § 482 BNSS
  • 📌 FIR Quashing → § 528 BNSS
  • 📌 Maintenance → § 144 BNSS
  • 📌 Police Notice → § 35 BNSS
Replaced Evidence Act 1872
Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam
BSA · Act 47 of 2023
Defines what counts as evidence.
  • 📌 WhatsApp chats ✅ primary evidence
  • 📌 Call recordings ✅ primary evidence
  • 📌 Emails & SMS ✅ primary evidence
  • 📌 Certificate required § 63(4) BSA
Filed before July 1, 2024? → IPC + CrPC + Evidence Act apply.
Filed on/after July 1, 2024? → BNS + BNSS + BSA apply.

🆘 If You're Being Arrested

⚠
Stay calm. Do NOT argue, resist, or sign anything.
1
Say nothing without your lawyer

"I want to speak with my lawyer before answering any questions."

2
Call NALSA for a free lawyer

Dial 15100 — free legal aid is your constitutional right under Article 22.

3
Apply for Anticipatory Bail (BNSS § 482)

If not yet arrested — file before arrest at Sessions Court. Can be done same day.

4
Write everything down

Names, times, what was said. Every detail matters for your case.
