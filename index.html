Effective July 1, 2024

India's New Criminal Laws

Three codes replaced 160-year-old colonial laws. If your case was filed after July 1, 2024, these apply to you.

Replaced IPC 1860
Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita
BNS · Act 45 of 2023

Defines what is a crime.

Key changes:
  • 📌 Old 498A → Now § 85 BNS
  • 📌 Old 406 → § 316 BNS
  • 📌 Old 182 → § 216 BNS
  • 📌 Old 211 → § 250 BNS
Replaced CrPC 1973
Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita
BNSS · Act 46 of 2023

Defines how crimes are investigated and how bail works.

Key changes:
  • 📌 Anticipatory Bail → § 482 BNSS
  • 📌 FIR Quashing → § 528 BNSS
  • 📌 Maintenance → § 144 BNSS
  • 📌 Police Notice → § 35 BNSS
Replaced Evidence Act 1872
Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam
BSA · Act 47 of 2023

Defines what counts as evidence.

Key changes:
  • 📌 WhatsApp chats ✅ primary evidence
  • 📌 Call recordings ✅ primary evidence
  • 📌 Emails & SMS ✅ primary evidence
  • 📌 Certificate needed § 63(4) BSA
Which law applies to your case?
✅ Filed before July 1, 2024 → IPC + CrPC + Indian Evidence Act apply
✅ Filed on or after July 1, 2024 → BNS + BNSS + BSA apply

🆘 Emergency Legal Help

⚠
If you are being arrested: Stay calm. Do NOT argue. Do NOT sign anything. Ask for a lawyer immediately.
What to do right now:
1
Say nothing without your lawyer

Tell police: "I want to speak with my lawyer before answering any questions."

2
Call NALSA for free legal aid

Dial 15100 — free legal aid is your constitutional right (Article 22).

3
Apply for Anticipatory Bail (BNSS § 482)

If not yet arrested — file before arrest at Sessions Court. A lawyer can do this today.

4
Document everything

Write down names, times, events. Screenshot all messages. Every detail matters.
